# Copywriting Skill

## When to use
- Writing landing page copy, headlines, CTAs
- Creating email sequences, ad copy, social posts
- Reviewing and improving existing marketing copy
- Structuring pages for conversion

## Headline formulas
1. **[Benefit] without [pain]**: "Scale your team without the hiring headaches"
2. **[Number] [audience] use [product] to [outcome]**: "10,000+ teams ship faster with X"
3. **How [audience] [achieves goal]**: "How top SaaS teams cut churn by 40%"
4. **[Question that triggers curiosity]**: "What if onboarding took 5 minutes?"
5. **[Bold claim + proof]**: "The fastest way to build landing pages. 200+ templates."

## CTA rules
- **Action verb + benefit**: "Start saving time", "Get your free report"
- **First person**: "Start my free trial" > "Start your free trial"
- **Urgency when real**: "Join 500 teams this week" (not fake scarcity)
- **Reduce friction**: "No credit card required", "Free forever plan"

## Copy structure (AIDA)
1. **Attention**: Headline that hooks
2. **Interest**: Subheadline with context
3. **Desire**: Benefits, social proof, outcomes
4. **Action**: Clear CTA with reduced risk

## Benefit writing
- ❌ Feature: "Real-time collaboration engine"
- ✅ Benefit: "Edit together, ship faster — no more version conflicts"
- Formula: "[What it does] so you can [outcome]"

## Tone guidelines
- **Conversational**: Write like you talk, not like a manual
- **Specific**: Numbers > adjectives ("40% faster" > "much faster")
- **Short sentences**: Average 12-15 words
- **Active voice**: "We built X" > "X was built by us"

## Common pitfalls
- Feature-dumping without benefits
- Multiple CTAs competing for attention
- Vague claims without proof
- Writing for everyone (talk to one persona)

## Tips for AI Agents
- Always ask about the target audience and single desired action.
- Write 3-5 headline variations for A/B testing.
- Default to benefit-first, not feature-first copy.
